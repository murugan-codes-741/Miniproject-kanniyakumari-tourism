The Goddess at the Edge of India

The Bhagavathy Amman Temple is one of the 108 Shakti Peethas of India and is dedicated to Goddess Kanyakumari, a form of Goddess Parvathi. The temple stands at the very tip of the Indian subcontinent, with the ocean on three sides. The nose ring (Mookuthi) of the Goddess is said to be so brilliantly jewelled that it can be seen from the sea, and ships once used it as a navigation landmark. The eastern gateway of the temple remains closed to prevent the jewel's light from distracting sailors.

Temple Rules

  • Men must wear dhoti or mundu (no pants inside)
  • Women must wear saree or churidar
  • Photography strictly prohibited inside
  • Mobile phones must be kept outside
  • Non-Hindus: allowed in outer premises only

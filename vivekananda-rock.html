The Rock of Enlightenment

Vivekananda Rock Memorial stands on a small island about 500 metres off the coast of Kanniyakumari. It was here that Swami Vivekananda swam to meditate in December 1892, before his historic address at the Parliament of Religions in Chicago. The memorial, built in 1970, beautifully blends Tamil and traditional Indian architecture. The main hall features a grand statue of Swami Vivekananda and the walls are carved with inspiring quotes.

Traveler Tips

  • Reach ferry point by 7:00 AM to avoid long queues
  • Remove footwear before entering the mandapam
  • Photography allowed outside, not inside halls
  • Carry water — no shops on the rock
  • Allow 1.5 to 2 hours for the full visit
